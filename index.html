Detailed Itinerary

  • Intercontinental flight from Amsterdam (AMS) to São Paulo (GRU)
  • Arrival in São Paulo, private transfer from the airport to accommodation with English-speaking assistance.
  • **Accommodation:** Pullman São Paulo Guarulhos Airport (1 night, L&B)

Day 2: Thursday, July 24 – São Paulo to Bonito

  • Shuttle transfer from hotel to São Paulo airport.
  • Local flight from São Paulo (GRU) to Campo Grande (CGR).
  • Pick up rental car (Ford EcoSport 2.0 a/c or similar, automatic transmission) at Campo Grande airport.
  • Drive from Campo Grande to Bonito (approx. 300 km / 4 hours).
  • **Accommodation:** Pousada Olho d'Água (3 nights, L&B)
  • Bonito is known for its crystal-clear rivers, rich in fish, ideal for snorkeling.

Day 3: Friday, July 25 – Bonito Activities

  • Snorkeling in the Rio da Prata, including equipment, lunch, entrance fees, and Portuguese-speaking guide. Expect to see various fish species like Piraputangas and Pacu.
  • Visit Buraco das Araras, a 124m deep, 500m wide crater, home to red-blue macaws and other birds. A lake with caimans is at the bottom.

Day 4: Saturday, July 26 – Bonito Activity

  • Snorkeling Lagoa Misteriosa (Mysterious Lake), including equipment and Portuguese-speaking guide. This sinkhole lake is one of Brazil's deepest underwater caves.

Day 5: Sunday, July 27 – Bonito to Pantanal

  • Drive from Bonito to your accommodation in the Pantanal (approx. 250 km / 4 hours).
  • **Accommodation:** Pousada Aguapé (3 nights, Full Board incl. activities)

Day 6-8: Monday, July 28 – Wednesday, July 30 – Pantanal Exploration

  • Explore the Pantanal, Brazil's second-most important natural area after the Amazon. It's a seasonal wetland rich in biodiversity.
  • Activities offered by the lodge: horseback riding, hiking, photo safaris, and boat trips. Expect to see diverse wildlife like caimans, giant anteaters, giant otters, anacondas, capybaras, and hyacinth macaws.

Day 8: Wednesday, July 30 – Pantanal to Campo Grande

  • Drive from Pantanal accommodation to Campo Grande (approx. 190 km / 3.5 hours).
  • Return rental car in Campo Grande, near your hotel.
  • **Accommodation:** Hotel Vale Verde (1 night, L&B)

Day 9: Thursday, July 31 – Campo Grande to Foz do Iguaçu

  • Private transfer from hotel to Campo Grande airport with Portuguese-speaking driver.
  • Local flights: Campo Grande (CGR) to Campinas (VCP), then Campinas (VCP) to Foz do Iguaçu (IGU).
  • Private transfer from Foz do Iguaçu airport to hotel with English-speaking assistance.
  • **Accommodation:** Vivaz Cataratas Hotel Resort (3 nights, L&B)

Day 10: Friday, August 1 – Foz do Iguaçu (Brazilian Side)

  • Half-day visit to the Brazilian side of the Iguaçu Falls with private English-speaking guide. Includes transfers and entrance fees. The falls are best visited in the morning for optimal light.
  • Macuco safari boat trip: a spectacular journey to the bottom of the falls by speedboat, preceded by an open jeep ride through the national park.

Day 11: Saturday, August 2 – Foz do Iguaçu (Argentinian Side)

  • Full-day visit to the Argentinian side of the Iguaçu Falls with private English-speaking guide. Includes transfers and entrance fees. Experience the "Garganta del Diablo" (Devil's Throat) up close.

Day 12: Sunday, August 3 – Foz do Iguaçu to Rio de Janeiro

  • Private transfer from hotel to Foz do Iguaçu airport with English-speaking assistance.
  • Local flight from Foz do Iguaçu (IGU) to Rio de Janeiro (GIG).
  • Private transfer from Rio de Janeiro airport to hotel with English-speaking assistance.
  • **Accommodation:** Bossa Nova Ipanema Hotel (4 nights, L&B)
  • Rio de Janeiro, the 'Cidade Maravilhosa,' offers iconic sights like Sugarloaf Mountain and Christ the Redeemer, and vibrant beaches.

Day 13: Monday, August 4 – Rio de Janeiro Landmarks

  • Full-day excursion to Corcovado (Christ the Redeemer statue) and Sugarloaf Mountain with private English-speaking guide. Includes transfers, entrance fees, and lunch. Enjoy panoramic views of the city.

Day 14: Tuesday, August 5 – Rio de Janeiro Culture & Nightlife

  • Half-day bike tour with private English-speaking guide, cycling from Copacabana along Guanabara Bay towards Lapa and the historic center, passing iconic neighborhoods, cemeteries, and the famous Selarón Steps.
  • Carioca nightlife tour with private English-speaking guide, exploring less touristy spots with live music (Samba or Forró). Tour starts around 9:00 PM and lasts until approx. 1:00 AM.

Day 15: Wednesday, August 6 – Free Day in Rio

  • Free day to explore. Suggestion: visit the Santa Teresa neighborhood, the oldest part of the city, known for its beautiful villas, boutique hotels, Escadaria Selarón, and the Santa Teresa tram.

Day 16: Thursday, August 7 – Departure from Rio

  • Private transfer from hotel to Rio de Janeiro airport with English-speaking assistance.
  • Intercontinental flight from Rio de Janeiro (GIG) to Amsterdam (AMS).

Day 17: Friday, August 8 – Arrival in Amsterdam

  • Arrival in Amsterdam.
